Adisorn Supawatanakul is a director and partner at Teak. He is an industrial designer and design researcher who leads teams of clients to broaden their understanding of their customers and discover innovative opportunities. Adisorn held a director position at Conifer Research USA for 12 years. He works globally in both developed and developing markets with clients from a range of industries on a variety of project types including design of new products and services, communication design and business strategy.

A graduate of IIT Institute of Design, Adisorn received his masters degree in human-centered product design as well as a Bachelors of Architecture from King Mongkut's Institute of Technology Ladkrabang. He was a speaker at Creativities Unfolded 2012 by Thailand Creative & Design Center (TCDC). He is also a guest lecturer at KMUTT and Chula University, Thailand.

His clients have included Steelcase, Comcast, Samsung, Monitor Group, AkzoNobel, Coinstar, Gensler, RTKL, McDonalds, Swensen’s, The Pizza Company, Regional Transit Authority in Chicago , and many more. 

adisorn@teakresearch.com
Praima Israsena Na Ayudhya, Ph.D is a director and partner at Teak. Throughout her career as a designer, researcher and educator, she has worked to expand the applications of design research in identifying opportunities at the front-end of innovation. While working as a design researcher with and for organizations in the US and Thailand, Praima has received research awards from the Environmental Design Research Association, USA (1997), the Helen Hamlyn Centre for Design, at the Royal College of Art, UK (2009), and Thailand National Research Universities Summit (2013).

Praima organized and chaired the first Design Research Society International Conference in Asia, DRS 2012: Bangkok, in order to promote design research in the region. She is currently teaching Industrial Design, and an interdisciplinary program in Technopreneurship and Innovation Management at Chulalongkorn University, Thailand.

Praima holds a bachelor’s degree in Industrial Design (first-class distinction with gold medal) from Chulalongkorn University, an M.A. in Design & Environmental Analysis (Phi Beta Kappa) from Cornell University and a Ph.D. in Design from the Institute of Design, IIT, USA. 

praima@teakresearch.com
Chujit Treerattanaphan, Ph.D. is a director and partner at Teak. Her research expertise is in user-centered design, cognitive and design psychology, communication design, interface/interaction design, and service design. Chujit has done human-centered design research projects regarding brand identity, product and service development strategy, energy efficiency, sustainable communities, space planning, environmental design with leading companies and organizations in Thailand including Thai Life Insurance, Mahaphant, Thainox, Saijo Denki, NECTEC, and Ministry of Justice.

Chujit is also a lecturer in the Industrial Design Program at the School of Architecture and Design, King Mongkut University of Technology Thonburi (KMUTT), Thailand. Chujit received her Bachelor’s degree in Industrial Design from KMUTT Lat Krabang, a Master’s degree in Industrial Design from Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), and a Ph.D. in Human-Centered Design from Institute of Design, Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT).

chujit@teakresearch.com
Watcharapong Treerattanaphan is a director at Teak. He has nearly 20 years of experience in communication design including visual, graphic, interface/interaction, web graphic, information architecture and way-finding design. Wat helps clients to translate brand strategy and brand image into visual language and visual identities across-the-board, both digital and non-digital platforms (printed materials and signage systems). He has also worked with clients in the US and Thailand including Whirlpool (USA), Baxter (USA), Polycom (USA), Microsoft (USA), Amway (USA), King Power, Reuters (Thailand), Tesco Lotus, 711, Central Plaza, InterContinental Hotel and Novotel.

Wat started his early professional practice as an interface/interaction designer at Insight Product Development, Chicago, USA for 4 years. At Insight Product Development, he worked closely with product development and the user research team.He later continued his career in Thailand as a corporate identity and way-finding designer at G49 architecture firm.

Wat earned a Master's degree in Industrial Design at Rochester Institute of Technology, New York.

Kanatip Janvipas is an associate researcher at Teak. She brings her knowledge of product and experience design from her work with toy design. In 2012, she participated in a short-term exchange program with a scholarship to attend Shibaura Institute of Technology, Japan in order to complete research about perception of “Cuteness” and “Coolness” among children ages 6–12 years old. Also she participated in a workshop called “Training in Brain-Based Learning Toys” organized by OKMD and King Mongut University of Technology (KMUTT) which was a workshop designed to produce toys for children 3-6 years old by using “Brain-Based” theory in cooperation with designers, therapists, and manufacturers. Kanatip was chosen to be one of the Thailand representatives at I-CREATE 2013 in Korea under the subject of “KLA - Reading and Writing practice media and tools for learning disabled children 6-9 years old”. She also participated in a “Toy Design Innovation” workshop under the topic of “Multi-Generational Interaction Toys” where her team got honorable mention for their design during this workshop

Kanatip graduated from Industrial Design program, School of Architecture and Design (SoA+D), King Mongkut University Technology of Thonburi (KMUTT), Thailand
